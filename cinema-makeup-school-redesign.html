Cinema Makeup School Redesign

Role

  • UX Designer

Delivered

  • Low-Fi Wireframes

Tools Used

  • Figma

Overview

I was brought in by Inward Eleven to help rethink the UX on CMS’s existing wordpress site. The site needed a modern design aesthetic, and better optimization for mobile and responsive web users. The client did some market research to find educational sights they liked.

Initial Research

We dug into the existing SEO to understand how the site was working for existing users. We then walked through CMS’s market research, putting together initial ideas of changes to implement.

Problems To Solve

Meeting with the client, we discussed specific elements to change.

Clean, Modern Design

The site needed to be brought up to date to match current trends

Information Architecture

Redesign the menu, optimizing it for mobile. Clarify and re-organize sections of the menu to reduce clutter.

Selling The School and Images

Create a visual way to sell their instructors, the experience of their graduates, and breadth of that network.

Data Input

Many students contact the school and research them years ahead of potential enrollment. Those prospective students needed the ability to input their data and contact information, so the school could keep in contact with them.


After the meeting we had a map how the process should evolve, based on what was important. A responsive design, and delivering mocks for each size of mobile/tablet/desktop.

Design Solutions

Overall Redesign

Redesign key pages and create a template for future pages. We targeted the landing, forms, instuctor, course schedule, housing, blog, tuition and class pages

Mobile Optimization

Since the majority of users were on mobile devices, catering to them first was key. Typing on a phone is harder than a computer, so I found ways to ease that data input whenever possible with click wheels.

Research (and anyone who has used a phone) shows users fingers and hands primarily at the bottom of the phone, so I moved the menu on mobile to a bottom static position to ease potential user discomfort. I also created direct links via icons to the primary actions (home, apply etc).

Information Architecture

I reorganized the menu information to be more streamlined, and while it addressed the issues, the first desktop pass didn’t resemble the exact design what they wanted. So that needed a second pass to rethink and pull from one of their references, which then worked perfectly for the client.

Images - Selling The School

Particularly because the school sells a visual medium, these needed to be bold. Edge to edge image sections were created for mobile for still images of the faculty, and videos explaining the courses. A system was also created, setting things into a grid format, and setting a heirarchy within the faculty members. Because they cater to a younger audience. we also gave the user an ability to swipe between images/videos.

To show the story of their alumni success, charts were set up that would visualize the numbers they wanted to show.

End Result

I only focused on the low-fidelity structure, so full redesign is still in process. But the client was very excited with these designs. They were then handed off to a visual designer, hired to create animations specific to the brand for the final design (blood drippingto fill in chart numbers, monster hands as menu icons etc).

They really loved the forward thinking on mobile, of a menu pinned to the bottom, and the click wheels for data input ease. Several of the sites also had a very dynamic visual site, so I wanted to make sure if we used a similar format, that text readbility over images and video wouldn’t be an issue.

Getting good client direction is also incredibly helpful, so when the desktop menu wasn’t up to snuff for them, specifics as to what needed to change made it easier to implement the desired changes

Adding detail down to the little things, and brand knowledge creates a sense of comfort for the client. Creating “cheeky” UX copy felt more personalized than lorem ipsum text. Putting their site address in the browser bar further personalizes the design for the client.
