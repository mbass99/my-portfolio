About Me

Why I design

Design has been in the background of my interests for a long time, I just didn’t always realize how.

In high school, I designed golf courses on a computer program. Putting myself in the shoes of the players, I loved charting a challenging, yet fair and visually appealing path, through each hole.

That program lead me to initially becoming an Architecture major in college, before realizing designing didn’t want to design buildings. I then turned to my love of story and eventually graduated from Film School.

I spent the next two decades helping develop other people’s stories, but I was always wanting to do more.

During a UI/UX informational class which explained how the discipline involved research, making things look nice and functional, as well as simplify the experience for the user - I knew that was my path.

UI/UX combined that creative itch I used to scratch with that golf game, with my abilities to develop narrative content.

Importance Of Design

Design tells a story

When you use a product, you are on a journey, be that to learn information, listen to a song or watch a movie, or see someone’s personal pictures they posted.

Bad design = user frustration

Frustration means you and may give up the chase for what you’re looking to do, in favor of something that annoys you less than a broken journey.

Think of design as characters in a great TV show

You accompany these people week in and out on a journey together, because you’re connected to them, and their goals as characters.

What interests me?

Privacy & User Needs

Privacy is something I’m passionate about. So my kids have the opportunity to carve their own path, without having their entire life pre-mapped by any by product scrubbed from my data.

I’m a consumer, and try to put myself in the shoes of users when I create something. I strive for simple, logical and engaging design.
